Preview · Brand

A friendly platform with two voices: orange that moves, violet that holds.

The shape of the Awin brand is warm-paper-and-lilac surfaces, oversized italic-accented headlines, gradient-orange CTAs, deep violet for footers and dividers, and a single floating pill header that lets the page breathe. Pill-shaped everything. Almost no shadows. No iconography beyond a small fire wordmark and a few line glyphs. Hierarchy comes from semibold weight and gradient fills - never from drop shadows or stroke.

The voice in one paragraph

Awin's voice is capable and friendly. Sentences are short. Headlines lean on subject-plus-verb ("Your growth, your way"; "The right connections change everything"). The site uses informal-but-direct language: "Start growing," "Tell us who you are," "Join our global affiliate platform." The tone never gets shouty about AI, growth or transformation - claims are concrete and number-backed (30K+ brands, 1M+ partners, £15B+ revenue, 25+ years) rather than aspirational. When the company speaks about itself it says "we", when it speaks to the reader it says "you", and when it summarises the mutual benefit it says "everyone wins bigger."

Ten rules that hold the system together

01

One typeface: Inter.

Display, body, UI, button labels. Six weights loaded; semibold and bold do most of the work.

02

Sentence case for headlines.

Title case is reserved for product / programme names and proper nouns.

03

Orange is the only loud accent.

Use the gradient on hero CTAs and the solid orange for stat numbers and inline CTA links.

04

Purple holds the room.

Footer, section dividers, dark hero variants - never on body type or buttons. It is the calm background voice.

05

Lilac is the second surface.

Lilac-50 for tertiary panels, soft-orange for warmer call-outs. Both are quieter than white.

06

Pill everything.

Buttons, tags, header bar, tabs. The square corners belong to cards (1rem / 1.5rem radii) and hairline rules.

07

Almost no shadow.

The exception is the floating header (a soft popover shadow). Everything else holds shape with surface colour and a 1px border.

08

One small icon vocabulary.

The Awin fire wordmark, social marks in the footer, and a `→` for inline CTAs. Don't add Lucide / Heroicons.

09

Gradients are an Awin signature.

Six named gradients: orange, orange-hover, orange-active, pulse, purple, lilac, ava-glow. Use the named tokens, don't compose new ones.

10

Stats wear the gradient.

Big numbers (`30K+`, `£15B+`) get the gradient-orange text fill via `.awn-stat__value`. Body type stays solid `--awn-text`.

Do / don't

Do

  • Lead with concrete numbers: 30K+ brands, not a global network.
  • Pair the gradient-orange CTA with a quiet ghost or text link as its secondary.
  • Use lilac-50 to break a long page up - it's the workhorse alternate surface.
  • Italicise the second half of a headline when you want it to land softer ("Your growth, your way").
  • Trust semibold (600) for body emphasis - it's stronger than colour for in-prose lift.

Don't

  • Don't put orange on broad surfaces. It's an accent, not a wall colour.
  • Don't shadow cards or buttons. Use a 1px hairline + lift on hover.
  • Don't stack two gradients in one section - pick orange or purple, not both.
  • Don't use Title Case for headlines. Sentence case throughout.
  • Don't add an icon set. Brand glyph + → is the vocabulary.
