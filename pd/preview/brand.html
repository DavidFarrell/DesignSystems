PREVIEW · BRAND VOICE

Plain. Concrete. British. Quietly confident.

PD writes like a senior practitioner explaining the work over a cup of tea. The voice is plain, concrete, slightly British, never breathless. Sentences are short and load-bearing. The whole site reads as one calm conversation between people who have done the work and want to be useful, not impressive.

Casing

  • Headlines - sentence case. "Supporting organisations through radical transformation."
  • Eyebrow labels - ALL CAPS, LIGHTLY TRACKED in Brown 700.
  • Buttons - sentence case. "Get in touch", never "GET IN TOUCH".
  • Body - sentence case, British English spelling.

Exemplars (lifted from the live site)

"Supporting organisations through radical transformation."
"Digital Sovereignty: Choosing your Future in a Competitive World."
"PD selected by Bloomberg Philanthropies to help supercharge the impact of UK metro mayors."
"Our values expressed in action and outcomes. Read them here."

Do / don't

DO
  • Lead with the work, not the boast.
  • Name the programme and the outcome.
  • Use one plate accent per surface.
  • Keep hero typography genuinely large.
DON'T
  • Use "world-class", "leverage", "synergies", "ecosystem".
  • Mix two plate accents in one section.
  • Add drop shadows. Anywhere.
  • Pull in an icon set. Brown plus a → is the system.
